Event Details

{% if company.program == "afternoon" or company.program == "afternoon and evening"%}

Arrival and Preparations

There will be tea and coffee to welcome you, in the lobby you can pick up your parking ticket and name badge. You can scan the QR code in the lobby to access the schedules. During this time you can also prepare your standing table and your banner. 10 minutes before the official start, you will also be given a few instructions by the committee and you can answer any of your remaining questions.

Introduction to the afternoon program

By this time, 1st and 2nd year students will have arrived at the event as well. Everyone will gather in the main hall to start the event with an opening speech and presentation. Then, the students will be have a few minutes to get into groups and prepare for the speed dates.

Speed dates (1st and 2nd round)

You will be meeting with 1st and 2nd year student groups for a speed date. You can find the which student groups are assigned to which time slot on the schedule.

Speed dates (3rd and 4th round)

You will be meeting with 2nd year students only for a speed date.

Company market

This time is reserved for students to walk around freely and approach the companies that they are interested in and network.

Speed dates and pitches (5th round)

You will be meeting 2nd year students for a speeddate. During this last slot, the students are expected to perform a short 'elevator pitch' to you, like they would in an interview. You are expected to fill in the pitch form with your feedback, sign it and give it back to the student.

Closing of the afternoon program

Gather in the main hall and close the afternoon program with a speech from the committee.

Introduction to the evening program

By this time, 3rd and 4th year students will have arrived at the event. Everyone will gather in the main hall to start the event with an opening speech and presentation.

Four rounds of evening speed dates

You will be meeting with students individually in 1-on-1 or 2-on-1 speed dates, based on a schedule made before the event. The third year students will be doing their internship in a duo, which is why they will be doing their speed date together in the 2-on-1's. The schedule has been made in mind with yours and the stduent's preferences in specialization and language.

Borrel

You can use this time to chat, socialize, have a drink, and connect with students that have not had the chance to speak to you yet.

Closing speech.

We will give a closing speach to mark the end of the networking day. We have gifts for you, so dont leave without them!
